# EU Financial Reg Horizon Scanner — LinkedIn Launch Post

## Primary post

A CASP client missed a six-week-old ESMA Q&A update last month.

Their compliance team pays Thomson Reuters €40k a year. The update was in the digest. It sat buried on page 11.

Reading every BaFin, ESMA, EBA, Commission, and Bundesbank publication is a full-time job. Finding the three sentences that touch your specific licence portfolio is a different job altogether.

So I built the tool I wanted to give them.

EU Financial Reg Horizon Scanner. Live for design partners this week.

What it does:

→ Ingests every publication from every EU and national authority touching crypto, payments, and digital assets

→ Tags each item against a five-axis taxonomy: regulation family, activity, licence type, topic, jurisdiction

→ Scores each item against your actual product map, your licences, your jurisdictions, your customer segments

→ Diffs updated documents at paragraph level, so the three sentences that moved in a 47-page Q&A revision arrive in your inbox already underlined

→ Routes every high-impact alert into a fixed-fee apexlaw remediation package, price visible on the alert

Why it lives inside the firm rather than as a standalone SaaS:

The services-as-software thesis I wrote about three weeks ago needs proof. A regulated legal boutique that ships software is a different competitor to Harvey or Legora. We carry the regulatory ground truth, the licence to advise on the output, and the supervisor relationships that make the analysis defensible in front of BaFin. Pure SaaS vendors do not.

First 25 design partners get six months at half price plus direct input into the taxonomy.

Drop "SCANNER" in the comments and I will send the brief.

## Posting playbook

**Goal:** announcement plus lead generation. Optimise for DMs over comments. The "SCANNER" trigger word in the CTA is the conversion mechanism.

**First 90 minutes:**

1. Stay on platform. Author stickiness drives the second-wave distribution.
2. Reply to every comment with a specific question that pulls the commenter into a real conversation (e.g. "What does your team currently use for horizon scanning? Curious whether you hit the same gap.").
3. For every "SCANNER" comment, send a DM within 10 minutes with a one-paragraph brief, a Calendly link for a 20-minute design partner call, and the half-price six-month offer. A reciprocal DM raises follow-on feed visibility by roughly 85 percent.
4. Pinned comment at minute 60 with the link to the original "Regulated Boutique Eats the Billable Hour" article. The pinned comment doubles dwell time and signals depth.

**Targeting beyond first-degree:**

Tag two or three people who will reshare for substantive reasons. Candidates from the existing fintech regulatory circle: Patrick Hansen, Lavan Thasarathakumar, Mikko Riikkinen if relevant, plus one in-house CASP compliance head who you already have a warm relationship with. Five tag ceiling.

Three to five hashtags only: `#MiCAR #DORA #Fintech #Compliance #LegalTech`. Niche beats broad.

**24-hour follow-up post:**

Quote the highest-quality comment from this launch and turn it into the next post. Compounds the algorithmic signal and gives the design partner cohort visible social proof.
